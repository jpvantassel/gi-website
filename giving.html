Giving

Two funds were setup to allow individuals or corporations to give specifically to the Geotechnical Engineering Program in the Department of Civil, Architectural and Environmental Engineering. For more information about each fund, please see the descriptions below.

Descriptions : This fund was created and is used to support the mission of the Student Chapter here at UT Austin. Since its inception, our student chapter has focused on broadening the minds of our geotechnical students, establishing strong connections with the students and members of the industry, and fostering a close-knit community among the students through various social events and group activities. This fund supports all Geo-Institute activities aimed solely to benefit our student members' lives.

To donate to the GI Student Chapter Fund, please visit: HERE

Donor Criteria : To provide support for the Geo-Institute Student Chapter to enhance the members' experience as a graduate student. The types of support may include but are not limited to, educational and industry weekly seminars, social events (Biannual Geotechnical Trivia, GI Tailgates, Graduation Parties, etc.), travel expenses for current student members to local conferences or lectures, and other expenditures for the direct benefit of graduate students members of GI. The UT Austin Geo-Institute Student Chapter officers shall decide the expenditure of funds.

Descriptions : Friends, alumni and colleagues of Roy Olson have joined together to create the Roy E. Olson Professorial Excellence Fund in Civil Engineering.  This endowment fund, which has a goal of $50,000, will be established in honor of a man who has given so much of himself to the lives of his students and colleagues. This fund supports graduate students in geotechnical engineering in the Civil Engineering Department.

To donate to the Olson Endowed Fund, please visit: HERE

Donor Criteria : To provide support for graduate students in Geotechnical Engineering in the Civil Engineering Department. The types of support may include, but are not limited to, direct payment of stipends in the forms of fellowships or assistantships, tuition and fees, travel expenses for potential or current graduate students, and other expenditures for the direct benefit of graduate students. Expenditure of funds shall be decided by the Director of the Geotechnical Engineering Program in conjunction with the Department Chair in the Civil Engineering Department at the University of Texas at Austin.
